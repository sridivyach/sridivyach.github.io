I am a PhD Student at Indian Institute of Science (IISc) with appointments in the Materials Research Center in the Division of Chemical Sciences.

My research interests lie within the broad area of Rechargeable Metal Ion Batteries. More specifically, my research spans investigation of Perovskite frameworks as anode, polyanionics as cathode hosts for Li/Na ion battery.

As the global energy demands are increasing, it is peak time to shift from a fossil fuel-based economy to a sustainable renewable one. This requires efficient energy storage systems like Lithium-ion batteries (LIBs) owing to the intermittent nature of these sources. Although current LIBs have revolutionized the portable electronics sector, the evolving EV and grid storage applications require higher energy and power densities. A dearth of Li resources also inspires search for alternate technologies like Sodium ion batteries. Commercial LIBs are made of graphite anodes and Transition metal oxide cathodes as intercalation hosts limited in capacity (~ 372 mAh /g) and voltage (~4V).

My work explores CAMs (conversion alloying materials) like AxBi1-xTiO3; (A = Na, K) type perovskite oxides for anodes providing very high capacities and LiMSO4F; (M = Fe, Co, Ni, Mn, Zn) type Metal fluoro-sulfates for cathodes providing high cell voltage. Following are the key aspects of the investigation:

  1. Synthesis of metastable phases by wet chemical routes
  2. Structural and Physical characterization of synthesized phases
  3. Study of the underlying charge storage mechanism
  4. Probing and mitigating the causes for cell failure
